**Implication for Pathway B:** When Sandy Hook observed water level
plus local enhancement (~+1.0 ft) exceeds these grate elevations, bay
water can flow up through the storm system. Our empirical 6.6 ft MLLW
Sandy Hook flood threshold corresponds to water at 342 Bay reaching
~7.6 ft MLLW = 4.78 NAVD88 — above all the local grate elevations.

## BFE / DFE context (from Forerunner, not from this PDF)

| Reference | NAVD88 | MLLW |
|---|---|---|
| Base Flood Elevation (FEMA AE, 1% chance) | 11.0 | 13.82 |
| Design Flood Elevation | 12.0 | 14.82 |
| Hurricane Sandy peak (Sandy Hook gauge) | 10.49 | 13.31 |
| 342 Bay Ave curb (best estimate) | 4.78 | 7.60 |
| Empirical flood onset at 342 Bay (back-calculated) | 3.78 | 6.60 |

So your curb sits about 6 ft below BFE. A 1% chance flood event would put
roughly 6 ft of water on Bay Avenue at your location — slightly worse
than Sandy 2012 (which the Borough's table records as 7 ft of water on
Bay Ave, suggesting BFE may be a touch low for your specific spot).

## What's worth extracting next

1. **Drawing C 100 visual check at 342 Bay Ave** — is the curb labeled
   5.30 specifically, or 4.78?
2. **Bay Ave elevations** — Phase 1 didn't reconstruct Bay Ave, but the
   survey may have included it. Worth checking other drawings or
   requesting from Borough.
3. **Connection to storm system map** — the borough's storm drain
   layout, showing which inlets connect to which outfall and where the
   outfalls discharge into the bay. Stephen Winters (Floodplain
   Administrator) would have this.
4. **Construction status** — was the reconstruction completed in 2024?
   If yes, current curb is at the design elevation. If still pending,
   the actual current curb may be lower (existing pre-construction
   elevations are mostly 0.3–0.8 ft below the design values).
