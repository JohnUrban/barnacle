# Bay Ave Barnacle — Flood Prediction Model v0.6

**Target:** Predict whether 342 Bay Ave (Highlands NJ) will flood in
the next 24 hours, with per-landmark depth estimates at 9 named
landmarks around the property.

- Added 9th landmark at the lowest observed point — a storm grate

## Core formula (unchanged from v0.5)

```
# Translate Sandy Hook gauge to local water level at 342 Bay Ave
LOCAL_ENHANCEMENT = +0.40 ft                 # empirically fit, 4-6 events
water_at_342_MLLW = Sandy_Hook_obs_MLLW + LOCAL_ENHANCEMENT
water_at_342_NAVD88 = water_at_342_MLLW - 2.82

# Per-landmark depth (inches)
depth_lowest_sentinel_grate = max(0, water_at_342_NAVD88 - 3.60) * 12   # NEW
depth_lowest_road_corner    = max(0, water_at_342_NAVD88 - 3.64) * 12
depth_gutter_walkway        = max(0, water_at_342_NAVD88 - 3.78) * 12
depth_corner_grate          = max(0, water_at_342_NAVD88 - 3.91) * 12
depth_curb                  = max(0, water_at_342_NAVD88 - 4.16) * 12
depth_road_middle           = max(0, water_at_342_NAVD88 - 4.36) * 12
depth_intersection          = max(0, water_at_342_NAVD88 - 4.54) * 12
depth_lawn_step             = max(0, water_at_342_NAVD88 - 4.58) * 12
depth_porch_step            = max(0, water_at_342_NAVD88 - 5.08) * 12

# Rain amplification (saturating, applied when peak rain >= 0.1"/hr)
rain_add = 8.0 * tanh(peak_rain_rate_in_hr)
# Sub-curb landmarks + curb + road: full rain_add (water pools at street)
depth_lowest_sentinel_grate += rain_add
depth_lowest_road_corner    += rain_add
depth_gutter_walkway        += rain_add
depth_corner_grate          += rain_add
depth_curb                  += rain_add
depth_road_middle           += rain_add
# Intersection (crown), lawn step (slight slope), porch step (flash-flood
# from up-slope per Oct 30 calibration) get attenuated rain_add:
depth_intersection += max(0, rain_add - 2)
depth_lawn_step    += max(0, rain_add - 4)
depth_porch_step   += max(0, rain_add - 4)

# Cold weather override (drains ice-locked)
if mean_air_temp_72h < 32°F and Sandy_Hook < 8.0:
    all depths = 0
```

The shortcut: at any Sandy Hook reading X (MLLW), water at 342 Bay sits
at `(X − 2.42)` ft NAVD88. So Sandy Hook threshold for landmark Y NAVD88
is just `Y + 2.42`.

involved.

**Hourly vs instantaneous note for Sandy-class events.** NOAA's
hourly_height product is a centered-hour averaged value; the 6-min
product captures instantaneous peaks. Hurricane Sandy peaked at
**12.03 ft on hourly_height** but **13.31 ft on the 6-min product** —
a ~10% gap at the extreme tail. A real-time forecast that reaches
12.0 ft on hourly products IS a Sandy-class event; don't anchor
expectations on the famous 13.31 number when comparing to hourly
forecasts. The historical-stats GEV fit uses hourly_height annual
maxima, so Sandy reads as 12.03 there.

## Three pathways

### Pathway A — Direct overtopping (rare, catastrophic)

Bay water rises directly to street and lawn elevations.

- **Onset:** Sandy Hook ≥ 6.58 ft MLLW (water tops curb at walkway)
- **Moderate at 342 Bay:** Sandy Hook ≥ 7.00 ft (water on lawn step)
- **Porch step reached:** Sandy Hook ≥ 7.50 ft (combined w/ rain on Oct 30)
- **Severe at 342 Bay:** Sandy Hook ≥ 7.58 ft (~1 ft over curb)
- **Catastrophic:** Sandy Hook ≥ 11.0 ft MLLW (BFE — 100-year flood)
- **Sandy 2012:** Sandy Hook 13.31 ft MLLW peak (6-min product) / 12.03 ft on
  hourly_height. The 13.31 figure is what historical accounts cite. About
  7 ft on Bay Ave at peak per Borough table.

### Pathway B — Storm drain back-flow (the workhorse)

Storm drain outfalls below Bay Ave become submerged in the bay;
back-pressure pushes water up through inlets onto the street. The +0.40 ft
"local enhancement" between Sandy Hook gauge and water at 342 Bay is
largely Pathway B at work — bay water arrives at the street before the
gauge reading alone would predict.

The lowest visible grate (the new 3.6 NAVD88 sentinel on Central Ave south)
is where this first becomes observable. The Bay+Central corner grate at
3.91 NAVD88 is the next-emerging point. Inlet grate elevations near 342 Bay
range 3.91–4.22 NAVD88 (Sandy Hook 6.33–6.64). Field check 2026-05-18
confirmed the Bay+Central corner grate sits slightly above the gutter line
(3.78), so water emerges from grates then flows downhill to pool at the
gutter — which is why the gutter (3.78) is the practical "water on the
street outside the walkway" threshold.

### Pathway C — Rainfall amplification

Heavy rain coinciding with high tide adds depth on top of A/B. Drains
can't outflow against bay back-pressure, so rain pools.

- **Trigger:** Peak hourly rainfall ≥ ~0.4 in/hr during ±2 h of high tide
- **Magnitude:** `8 × tanh(peak_rain_rate)` inches added at curb / road
  middle and at all sub-curb landmarks (sentinels, grates, gutter); less
  at the intersection (sheds via crown) and lawn step (sheds via slight
  slope); attenuated by 4" at the porch step
- **Calibration source:** Dec 19 2025 (0.44"/hr, +~5" added) and Oct 30
  2025 (1.45"/hr, brought water to the porch step despite modest tide
  contribution — see "Oct 30 calibration" below)

**Oct 30 2025 calibration of the porch-step rain term.** Observed Sandy
Hook 7.57 ft + 1.45 in/hr rain. Tide+surge alone would give only ~0.8"
at the porch step (5.08 NAVD88). User reported water "rising to the first
step level." The rain term `rain_add - 4` at the porch step lifts the
prediction to ~4", roughly consistent with the observation. Physical
mechanism: flash-flood / sheet flow arrives horizontally from up-slope
(Waterwitch Ave et al.) and from upstream Navesink/Shrewsbury river rise
— not vertical pooling on the lawn. Single-event calibration; rough above
the curb level.

## Cold-weather override

When `mean_air_temp_72h < 32°F` and Sandy Hook < 8.0 ft, set predicted
depths to zero.

**Mechanism:** Ice at storm drain outfalls blocks the Pathway B back-flow
that produces the +0.40 ft local enhancement. Without that pathway, bay
water at 7+ ft MLLW stays in the bay rather than emerging on Bay Avenue.

**Evidence:** Feb 22–23 2026, observed tide 7.19 ft + strong onshore winds
+ no flooding at 342 Bay confirmed by overnight vigil. Borough issued no
post-event coastal flood advisory.

**Caveat:** Single confirmed observation. Confidence in the override
threshold (72h, 32°F) is low. More cold-weather high-tide events will
refine. HANDOFF item 10 + the daily spot-check prompt now explicitly
calls out cold-lockout calibration opportunities.

## External threshold validation

Three independent sources converge on "minor coastal flooding starts at
~6.6 ft MLLW Sandy Hook," cross-validating the model from very different
methodologies:

| Source | Threshold (ft MLLW) | Methodology |
|---|---|---|
| User's empirical curb onset | 6.58 | 4 firsthand flood-event labels at 342 Bay |
| NWS Mt Holly "Minor Coastal Flooding" | 6.70 | Regional NWS definition (Eastern Monmouth) |
| Sandy Hook Tidal Dashboard "Minor" | 6.70 | Same as NWS; displays this on live UI |

The three agree within **0.12 ft (~1.4 inches)**. The user's hyperlocal
empirical curb is ~1.4 inches more sensitive than NWS's regional Minor —
meaning a property like 342 Bay registers slightly more events than the
dashboard's count, but not dramatically more. Per the historical-stats
project, going from 6.70 → 6.58 ft increases the count by ~20% in
matched windows.

**The dashboard's monthly flood counts (over 1996–2025) are reproducible
to within 2%** using a 6.70 ft threshold and the same NOAA hourly_height
data — independent confirmation that the dashboard is using NWS standards
and not some idiosyncratic definition.

## Long-term context (historical-stats project, 2026-05-18)

A separate analysis pulled NOAA Sandy Hook hourly data 1910–2026
(`history/` in repo) and confirmed:

- **All 4 labeled events from the original v0.5 set match historical
  pull values exactly.** No model recalibration implied. The dataset is
  self-consistent.
- **SLR at Sandy Hook: 4.29 mm/yr (1932–2024)** vs NOAA's published
  4.05 mm/yr — agreement within 6%, confirming the gauge and processing
  chain are sound.
- **Recent acceleration: 5.45 mm/yr post-1980.** About 1.35× the
  long-term rate. Forward-projecting at 5.45 mm/yr: ~0.5 ft additional
  rise by 2055. At that point, today's routine 6.6 ft high tides will
  wet the lawn step at 342 Bay — not just the curb.
- **Flood-day frequency (at curb 6.58 ft) recent decades:** 38 (2010s),
  44 (2020s). Up ~15× from the 1950s rate.
- **100-year return level: 10.57 ft MLLW** (95% CI 9.5–11.8). At the
  user's curb, ~48 inches of water from tide alone, before any rain
  amplification.

See `history/reports/flood_history_report.md` and
`history/RESULTS_HANDOFF.md` for full details.

## What might break this model

1. **Local enhancement isn't truly constant.** Currently 0.40 ft fits all 4 confirmed events to within ±0.05 ft. May vary with wind direction, storm category, or lunar phase. Becomes detectable with 8–10 events.
2. **Rain term is fit with one data point at the porch level** (Oct 30).  The 8·tanh saturation curve plus `rain_add - 4` attenuation at the porch is plausible but speculative above the curb.
3. **Cold lockout based on single observation** (Feb 22–23). The 32°F / 72h threshold is a guess.
4. **Phase 1 construction status unknown.** If curb has been reconstructed to design (+0.04 ft), all thresholds shift up by ~0.5 inch — within noise.
5. **Sea-level rise is accelerating.** Historical-stats project shows
   ~5.45 mm/yr post-1980, vs the long-term 4.05 mm/yr. Will silently
   shift the +0.40 enhancement upward by ~0.07 ft per decade (faster
   than the 0.04 ft/decade long-term rate). Re-calibrate every 5 years,
   not 10.
6. **The new 3.60 NAVD88 sentinel may behave differently** from the
   higher landmarks. It's further from the property and on Central Ave;
   the local enhancement +0.40 ft was calibrated at the curb. Whether
   it applies the same at the lowest sentinel is an open question —
   labeled observations will resolve.
