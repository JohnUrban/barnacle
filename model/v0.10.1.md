# Bay Ave Barnacle — Flood Prediction Model v0.10.1

**Promotion date:** 2026-07-18, after Event #5 supplied a clean measured
recession limb. **Production cutover stamp:** 2026-07-21; the constants below
were already live from July 18, but forecasts before this documentation
cutover were incorrectly stamped `v0.10`.

v0.10.1 is a measured-parameter refinement of the v0.10 dynamic pluvial
tank. It does not change the model structure. The tide pathway, 18-landmark
ladder, stage-storage curve, head-dependent drainage, wind adjustment, NWS
inputs, and static scenario bracket carry forward unchanged. The superseded
v0.10 fit is preserved at [`archive/v0.10.md`](archive/v0.10.md); older model
families are also under `archive/`.

## What changed from v0.10

Event #5 (2026-07-18) provided a rain-free recession interval from 16:03 to
16:40 local. That limb measured `TANK_KOUT = 3.50 /h`; continuing grate jets
mean 3.50/h is a lower bound on unconstrained drainage. With `k_out` pinned,
`K`, `gamma`, and lag were jointly refit against the existing July 6 and July
9 hydrographs. Joint RMS improved from 1.44 to 1.32 inches.

| Parameter | v0.10 | v0.10.1 production | Basis |
|---|---:|---:|---|
| `TANK_K` | 1.265e6 | **1.296e6** cell-in/h | joint refit |
| `TANK_GAMMA` | 0.70 | **0.78** | joint refit |
| `TANK_KOUT` | 3.12/h | **3.50/h** | measured Event #5 recession |
| `TANK_LAG_MIN` | 14 min | **15 min** | joint refit; observed 14–20 min |

No Event #5 peak was added to the fit. The event supplied the independent
outflow measurement that justified pinning `k_out`; the two previously fitted
hydrographs remained the fit set.

## Dynamic pluvial tank

```text
dV/dt  = TANK_K · max(0, R(t − lag) − D(bay))^TANK_GAMMA
          − TANK_KOUT · V
stage  = stage_storage_curve(fill from tide-set base, +V)
water  = 3.52 + stage/12          (NAVD88)

TANK_K       = 1.296e6 cell-in/h at 1 in/hr net
TANK_GAMMA   = 0.78
TANK_KOUT    = 3.50 /h            (~17-minute reservoir e-folding)
TANK_LAG_MIN = 15 min
D(bay)       = head-dependent drain (0.25 in/hr below bay 3.0,
               tapering to 0 at bay 3.52)
```

The input is raw hourly NWS QPF for forecasts and observed MRMS PrecipRate for
nowcasts. The tank supplies lag, integration, and recession. QPF can smear or
miss convection, so validated tank physics is not the same as validated rain
input skill.

## Tide-keyed pathway

For each high tide:

```text
SH peak MLLW = astronomical tide + NWS projection or observed surge
water NAVD88 = SH peak MLLW + 0.00 − 2.82
depth at landmark = max(0, water − landmark NAVD88) × 12 inches
```

If live surge is unavailable, production now labels the calculation
`astronomical-only-degraded` rather than calling a zero departure surge
persistence. Offshore peak winds retain the separately reported −0.13 ft
expected-actual adjustment. The retired cold-lockout remains advisory only.

## Static scenarios

The map slider, rain calculator, and advisory scenarios use the tank's steady
state with the same v0.10.1 constants:

```text
V_steady = (TANK_K / TANK_KOUT) · net_rate^TANK_GAMMA
```

The saturating tanh estimate remains a labeled conservative comparison. The
v0.9 power-law remains available only as a legacy comparison; neither drives
the primary hydrograph.

## Calibration evidence and limitations

- Fit set: July 6 and July 9, 2026 tape-measured hydrographs.
- Independent checks inherited from v0.10: October 30 and December 19, 2025.
- Independent parameter measurement: Event #5 recession, July 18, 2026.
- Full Event #5 evidence and caveats:
  [`assets/observations/2026-07-18/README.md`](../assets/observations/2026-07-18/README.md).
- Fit implementation: [`history/scripts/tank_model_fit.py`](../history/scripts/tank_model_fit.py)
  documents the original v0.10 fit. The v0.10.1 measured constraint and
  refit result are recorded in the Event #5 evidence and production constants;
  a standalone pinned-refit script is still desirable for full reproducibility.

Known structural gaps remain deliberately untuned: antecedent catchment
wetting, bidirectional drains and hysteresis, a tilted recession pool,
recirculation between grates, a long hillside delivery tail, and the sidewalk
swale micro-basin. Future events should test these structures without silently
changing this version's constants.
