## Core formula (tide-keyed path — unchanged from v0.8)

```
LOCAL_ENHANCEMENT = 0.00 ft
MLLW_TO_NAVD88     = -2.82 ft

water_at_342_NAVD88 = Sandy_Hook_MLLW + LOCAL_ENHANCEMENT + MLLW_TO_NAVD88

if peak_rain_rate_in_hr > 0.1:          # rain in [-90min, +15min] of peak
    water_at_342_NAVD88 += (8.0 * tanh(peak_rain_rate_in_hr)) / 12.0

depth(landmark) = max(0, water_at_342_NAVD88 - landmark.elevation) * 12
```

Wind adjustment (v0.8, unchanged): offshore forecast winds at peak
(S/SSW/SW/WSW/SSE) → report a −0.13 ft "expected actual" line
alongside the main prediction.

## Pluvial model (NEW — v0.9-alpha)

```
base = max(bay_water_navd88, 3.52)         # street bowl bottom (grate_SW)
drains functional (bay < 3.52): lift = 1.40 · tanh(rate / 1.2)
drains blocked   (bay ≥ 3.52): lift = 8 · tanh(rate) / 12   # the v0.8 term

pluvial_water = base + lift     (rate > 0.1 in/hr)
```

Calibration (2 events):

| Event | Bay water | Rain rate | Model | Observed |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---|
| 2026-07-06 pure pluvial | ~2.6 | ~1.7 in/hr burst (FITTED — observed rate unknown) | 4.76 | 4.77–4.84 (peak band) |
| 2025-10-30 compound | 4.81 (SH 7.63) | 1.45 in/hr (recorded) | 5.41 | ≥ 5.25 |

Why free-drain lift exceeds blocked-drain lift: bowl-vs-plain
geometry (see code comment). Known warts: regime discontinuity at
bay = 3.52; the 1.7 in/hr burst is a fitted assumption; no timing
model (the banner says "a burst today could do X," not when).
Stage-storage curve + observed rain input are the v0.9-final work.

Surfaced ONLY in the pluvial advisory banner as ±3″-class scenario
estimates; the tide-keyed table/JSON are untouched by it.

### v0.9-beta pluvial (2026-07-07 upgrade — volume-based, unified)

The two-regime closed form above is superseded in code by a single
continuous model: **rain delivers a saturating net volume
(`V_K · tanh((rate − 0.25)/1.2)`, drains eat the first 0.25 in/hr),
and depth follows by filling the measured stage-storage curve**
(`history/data/stage_storage_curve.csv`, from the 96-point heat-map
surface) upward from the base level the tide has set.

Two-source principle (user): the bay is an effectively infinite
reservoir → tidal flooding is LEVEL-driven (tide-keyed path unchanged,
no volume math); rain is a FINITE source → volume-filled through the
curve. Compound flooding is therefore additive but SUB-LINEAR — the
same rain adds fewer inches from a higher base because the area is
larger there (volume per inch at curb stage ≈ 13× grate stage; ≈ 35×
at lawn-step stage).

**Head-dependent drainage (2026-07-07, same-day refinement)**: the
drain subtraction D is no longer constant — capacity collapses as
the bay submerges the outfall. Full 0.25 in/hr with the bay below
3.0 NAVD88, ramping linearly to zero at 3.52 (grate tops =
backwatered). Fixes the Oct 30 double-count (drains were blocked,
yet constant-D subtracted 0.25 from its rain rate). Emergent
behavior: light rain that "drains fine" at low tide lifts water
when the bay is high — physically right.

Parameter provenance (be honest here): **V_K is the only fitted
parameter** (7/6 anchor: 1.7 in/hr from an empty bowl → +15.4″;
bay was ~2.6 → drains full-capacity, so the anchor's net rate is
unchanged by head-dependence). **0.25 is a judgment number**
bracketed by weak evidence (0.09-smeared QPF didn't pool; 0.44 with
blocked drains flooded), not ground truth — V_K partially absorbs
its error. **1.2 (tanh scale) is a placeholder** from v0.9-alpha,
never independently identified; Michaelis–Menten is a candidate
replacement form. **3.0 (drainage knee) is a placeholder** pending
the borough drainage map / more compound events.

Cross-checks (zero additional fitting):
- Oct 30 (base 4.81, rate 1.45): 5.24 NAVD88 vs observed ≥
  5.25–5.27 (constant-D gave 5.20; v0.9-alpha was 2″ over).
- **Dec 19 2025 (base 4.04, rate 0.44): 4.51 NAVD88 vs landmark
  anchor band [4.36, 4.54] — INSIDE the band.** The event every
  prior version failed on now fits: modest rain that mattered only
  because the bay had blocked the drains.

Regime discontinuity at bay = 3.52: gone (continuity verified).
Fallback to the alpha closed form if the curve CSV is missing.
Caveats: connected-pool assumption overstates low-volume cases;
curve is a lower bound above ~+16″.

**7/6 forcing MEASURED (2026-07-07, MRMS radar —
`history/scripts/mrms_point_rain.py`, Iowa State mtarchive
archives)**: peak 2-min rate **2.95 in/hr at 11:12 ET** (hillside
cells to 3.06), ~2 in/hr sustained 11:04–11:20, hour ending 12:00
ET = 0.94″, storm total 1.60″. Your measured water peak at 11:34 =
**~20 min lag** behind peak rain (hillside concentration time).
Catchment amplification quantified: filling the bowl to +15.4″
takes ≈ 5.9″ of rain-equivalent over the peak wetted footprint, but
only ≈ 0.45″ fell during the 39-min rise → **≥ ~13× amplification**
(lower bound; mapped area is bounded). The model's "1.7 in/hr
effective burst" is thus a proxy conflating true rate (~2–3) with
partial-duration effects; V_K absorbs the difference.

### v0.9-gamma pluvial (2026-07-07 — dual input models, both reported)

User directive: "keep the tanh model and add the power-law runoff
model, and report both." Two rate→volume forms now share the same
head-dependent drainage and stage-storage fill:

- **POWER (primary)**: `V = K_pow · net_rate^γ`, fitted to all THREE
  anchors in the model's input space. Self-calibrating from the
  curve at load: γ = 0.914, K_pow ≈ 412k. Near-linear, NO
  saturation. It fits every anchor at least as well as tanh
  (Dec 19: 4.449 vs band-mid 4.45; Oct 30: 5.246 vs obs ≥5.25;
  7/6: 4.814 vs 4.77–4.84).
- **TANH (co-reported)**: the v0.9-beta form, unchanged. Kept as
  the saturating alternative, NOT as a fallback.

The two agree within ~1″ across the calibrated range (0.4–1.7
in/hr net) and diverge in extrapolation — at a 3 in/hr burst the
power law is +3.4″ deeper at low tide, +6.1″ at 4 in/hr — exactly
where there is no data (the Sandy question). Surfaces report the
spread as the model uncertainty: series carries
`pluvial_navd88` (primary) + `pluvial_navd88_tanh` (additive
field); `pluvial_risk` carries both potential levels; the chart /
widget band top is the HIGHER of the two; the advisory banner
prints each scenario as a [min–max] bracket. The primary drives
the combined `water_navd88` line and flood windows. The
v0.9-alpha closed form remains the only true FALLBACK (used solely
if the stage-curve CSV is missing). Provenance: γ/K_pow
provisional (n=3, 1 dof); refit per event. The MRMS C·(R−D)·T
analysis motivates the shape (delivery efficiency grows with
intensity; Michaelis–Menten saturates the wrong way).

**All three rain anchors measured (same day)**: Oct 30 2025 — burst
peak 2.71 in/hr at 14:50 ET, 4 min before the 14:54 tide peak
(hill-box max 4.18, strongest cell on record over the catchment);
Dec 19 2025 — 1.83 in/hr spike at 07:00 ET then steady 0.2–0.45.
Fitting V = C·(R−D)·T (stage-curve fill volume vs integrated MRMS
net input): C = 614k / 449k / 210k cell-units for 7/6 / Oct 30 /
Dec 19 — order-stable (amplification in every event) but growing
with rain intensity → intensity-dependent runoff fraction
(threshold/power-law) is the indicated upgrade path; constant-C
linear and Michaelis–Menten (saturates the wrong way) are not. The
tanh proxy remains serviceable: V_K is calibrated at the violent
end and all three events fit in stage space. MRMS sweep of every
OTHER observed event day (5/18, 5/19, 5/30, 5/31, 6/14, 6/15,
Apr 17–18 2026, Aug 21 2025, Feb 22–23 2026): all dry — the tide
calibration is unconfounded by rain, and rain does not explain the
Apr 17/18 memory-depth under-predictions.

## What might break this model

1. **Pluvial timing is unmodeled** — the banner is categorical.
2. ~~The 1.7 in/hr burst is fitted, not measured~~ **MEASURED
   2026-07-07 via MRMS**: true peak 2.95 in/hr, ~2 sustained; the
   1.7 "effective rate" stands as a calibrated proxy (see beta
   addendum). Remaining: refit the model on true rates + durations
   (V = C·(R−D)·T candidate).
3. **QPF smears convective bursts** (~6-h buckets) — PoP + wording
   triggers carry the banner; radar nowcast is the real fix (9e.2).
4. ~~Regime discontinuity at bay = 3.52~~ **FIXED by v0.9-beta**
   (volume-fill + head-dependent drainage; continuity verified).
5. **Seasonality CSV thresholds are v0.6-era** — annual analytics
   refresh should re-run against v0.9 thresholds.
6. Wind rule, enhancement extrapolation above SH 7.3, cold lockout,
   SLR drift: carried from v0.8 unchanged.
