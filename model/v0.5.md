# Bay Ave Barnacle — Flood Prediction Model v0.5

**Target:** Predict whether 342 Bay Ave (Highlands NJ) will flood in
the next 24 hours, with per-landmark depth estimates.

**Changes from v0.4:** Arithmetic corrections to three landmark Sandy Hook
threshold values in the Hydraulic Interpretation table and Pathway A onset
levels. Python code (`flood_forecast_daily.py`) was already correct — only
the human-readable tables in v0.4 had errors. No model logic changes.

**Post-v0.5 corrections (2026-05-18):** Threshold context corrected after
the historical-statistics project produced independent validation —
see "External threshold validation" section below. The earlier
characterization of the Sandy Hook dashboard as using a 7.20 ft Minor
threshold was wrong; correct value is 6.70 ft (NWS standard), which is
a stronger validation of the model than originally credited.

**v0.5.1 addition (2026-05-18 PM):** Added a 5th landmark — "front
porch first step" at 5.08 ft NAVD88 (Sandy Hook threshold 7.50 ft) —
to enable severity stratification in the daily forecast and seasonal
context. Estimated as lawn step (4.58) + 6 inches; should be field-
measured to tighten.

**Local enhancement at porch step:** The +0.40 ft offset is applied at
this landmark same as the others. Physically the gauge-to-bay offset
still holds; whether bay-to-342-Bay matches exactly that high up is an
open question with no calibration data above ~7.6 ft MLLW.

**Rain term at porch step:** Receives `rain_add - 4` (same attenuation
as the lawn step). The physical mechanism is flash-flood / sheet flow
arriving horizontally from up-slope (Waterwitch Ave et al.) and from
upstream Navesink/Shrewsbury river rise — not vertical pooling on the
lawn. Calibrated against Oct 30 2025: observed Sandy Hook 7.57 ft +
1.45 in/hr rain, user reported water "rising to the first step level."
Tide+surge alone gives only 0.8" at the porch step; the rain term lifts
the model prediction to ~4" at the porch, roughly consistent with that
observation. Single-event calibration; rough above the curb level.

If anything in another document disagrees with v0.5, **v0.5 wins.**

## Core formula (unchanged from v0.4)

```
# Translate Sandy Hook gauge to local water level at 342 Bay Ave
LOCAL_ENHANCEMENT = +0.40 ft                 # empirically fit, 4 events
water_at_342_MLLW = Sandy_Hook_obs_MLLW + LOCAL_ENHANCEMENT
water_at_342_NAVD88 = water_at_342_MLLW - 2.82

# Per-landmark depth (in)
depth_curb       = max(0, water_at_342_NAVD88 - 4.16) * 12
depth_road       = max(0, water_at_342_NAVD88 - 4.36) * 12
depth_intersect  = max(0, water_at_342_NAVD88 - 4.54) * 12
depth_lawn       = max(0, water_at_342_NAVD88 - 4.58) * 12
depth_porch_step = max(0, water_at_342_NAVD88 - 5.08) * 12   # added v0.5.1

# Rain amplification (saturating, applied when peak rain >= 0.1"/hr)
rain_add = 8.0 * tanh(peak_rain_rate_in_hr)
depth_curb       += rain_add
depth_road       += rain_add
depth_intersect  += max(0, rain_add - 2)
depth_lawn       += max(0, rain_add - 4)
# Porch step gets no rain add: by the time water reaches it, tide dominates.

# Cold weather override (drains ice-locked)
if mean_temp_72h < 32°F and Sandy_Hook < 8.0:
    all depths = 0
```

The shortcut: at any Sandy Hook reading X (MLLW), water at 342 Bay sits
at `(X − 2.42)` ft NAVD88. So Sandy Hook threshold for landmark Y NAVD88
is just `Y + 2.42`.

These are all derived as `landmark_NAVD88 + 2.42`. No other arithmetic
involved.

**Hourly vs instantaneous note for Sandy-class events.** NOAA's hourly_height
product is a centered-hour averaged value; the 6-min product captures
instantaneous peaks. Hurricane Sandy peaked at **12.03 ft on hourly_height**
but **13.31 ft on the 6-min product** — a ~10% gap at the extreme tail.
A real-time forecast that reaches 12.0 ft on hourly products IS a Sandy-class
event; don't anchor expectations on the famous 13.31 number when comparing
to hourly forecasts. The same gap affects historical maximum-tide tables
that use hourly_height (the historical-stats project's GEV fit uses
hourly_height annual maxima, so Sandy reads as 12.03 there).

## Three pathways

### Pathway A — Direct overtopping (rare, catastrophic)

Bay water rises directly to street and lawn elevations.

- **Onset:** Sandy Hook ≥ 6.58 ft MLLW (water tops curb at walkway)
- **Moderate at 342 Bay:** Sandy Hook ≥ 7.00 ft (water on lawn step)
- **Severe at 342 Bay:** Sandy Hook ≥ 7.58 ft (~1 ft over curb)
- **Catastrophic:** Sandy Hook ≥ 11.0 ft MLLW (BFE — 100-year flood)
- **Sandy 2012:** Sandy Hook 13.31 ft MLLW peak (6-min product) / 12.03 ft on
  hourly_height. The 13.31 figure is what historical accounts cite. About
  7 ft on Bay Ave at peak per Borough table.

### Pathway B — Storm drain back-flow (the workhorse)

Storm drain outfalls below Bay Ave become submerged in the bay; back-pressure pushes water up through inlets onto the street. The +0.40 ft "local enhancement" between Sandy Hook gauge and water at 342 Bay is largely Pathway B at work — bay water arrives at the street before the gauge reading alone would predict.

Inlet grate elevations near 342 Bay are 3.91–4.22 NAVD88 (= Sandy Hook thresholds 6.33–6.64 MLLW). The empirical 6.58 onset matches: bay water is above the inlet grates by Sandy Hook ~6.4 ft, well before the curb is structurally overtopped.

### Pathway C — Rainfall amplification

Heavy rain coinciding with high tide adds depth on top of A/B. Drains can't outflow against bay back-pressure, so rain pools.

- **Trigger:** Peak hourly rainfall ≥ ~0.4 in/hr during ±2 h of high tide
- **Magnitude:** `8 × tanh(peak_rain_rate)` inches added at curb / road middle; less at the intersection (sheds via crown) and lawn (sheds via slight slope)
- **Calibration source:** Dec 19 2025 (0.44"/hr, +~5" added) and Oct 30 2025 (1.45"/hr)

## Cold-weather override

When `mean_air_temp_72h < 32°F` and Sandy Hook < 8.0 ft, set predicted depths to zero.

**Mechanism:** Ice at storm drain outfalls blocks the Pathway B back-flow that produces the +0.40 ft local enhancement. Without that pathway, bay water at 7+ ft MLLW stays in the bay rather than emerging on Bay Avenue.

**Evidence:** Feb 22–23 2026, observed tide 7.19 ft + strong onshore winds + no flooding at 342 Bay confirmed by overnight vigil. Borough issued no post-event coastal flood advisory.

**Caveat:** Single confirmed observation. Confidence in the override threshold (72h, 32°F) is low. More cold-weather high-tide events will refine.

## External threshold validation

Three independent sources converge on "minor coastal flooding starts at
~6.6 ft MLLW Sandy Hook," cross-validating the model from very different
methodologies:

| Source | Threshold (ft MLLW) | Methodology |
|---|---|---|
| User's empirical curb onset | 6.58 | 4 firsthand flood-event labels at 342 Bay |
| NWS Mt Holly "Minor Coastal Flooding" | 6.70 | Regional NWS definition (Eastern Monmouth) |
| Sandy Hook Tidal Dashboard "Minor" | 6.70 | Same as NWS; displays this on live UI |

The three agree within **0.12 ft (~1.4 inches)**. The user's hyperlocal
empirical curb is ~1.4 inches more sensitive than NWS's regional Minor —
meaning a property like 342 Bay registers slightly more events than the
dashboard's count, but not dramatically more. Per the historical-stats
project, going from 6.70 → 6.58 ft increases the count by ~20% in
matched windows.

**The dashboard's monthly flood counts (over 1996–2025) are reproducible
to within 2%** using a 6.70 ft threshold and the same NOAA hourly_height
data — independent confirmation that the dashboard is using NWS standards
and not some idiosyncratic definition. This was tested empirically by
Claude Code's historical-statistics project.

**Earlier confusion:** prior versions of the project documentation
claimed the dashboard used a 7.20 ft Minor threshold. This was wrong;
the actual operative threshold is 6.70 ft. The 7.20 figure appears to
have come from misreading static fallback values in the dashboard's
pre-JavaScript HTML rather than the live UI values.

## Long-term context (historical-stats project, 2026-05-18)

A separate analysis pulled NOAA Sandy Hook hourly data 1910–2026
(`history/` in repo) and confirmed:

- **All 4 labeled v0.5 events match historical pull values exactly.**
  No model recalibration implied. The dataset is self-consistent.
- **SLR at Sandy Hook: 4.29 mm/yr (1932–2024)** vs NOAA's published
  4.05 mm/yr — agreement within 6%, confirming the gauge and processing
  chain are sound.
- **Recent acceleration: 5.45 mm/yr post-1980.** About 1.35× the
  long-term rate. Forward-projecting at 5.45 mm/yr: ~0.5 ft additional
  rise by 2055. At that point, today's routine 6.6 ft high tides will
  wet the lawn step at 342 Bay — not just the curb.
- **Flood-day frequency (at curb 6.58 ft) recent decades:** 38 (2010s),
  44 (2020s). Up ~15× from the 1950s rate. The 1910s baseline is too
  thin (only 7 years of usable data) to be a reliable comparator.
- **100-year return level: 10.57 ft MLLW** (95% CI 9.5–11.8). At the
  user's curb, ~48 inches of water from tide alone, before any rain
  amplification. Hurricane Sandy at 13.31 ft is well above this — fit
  classifies it as a ~5000-year event, with the appropriate caveat that
  this is the fit's verdict, not a probability statement.

See `history/reports/flood_history_report.md` and
`history/RESULTS_HANDOFF.md` for full details.

## What might break this model

1. **Local enhancement isn't truly constant.** Currently 0.40 ft fits all 4 confirmed events to within ±0.05 ft. May vary with wind direction, storm category, or lunar phase. Becomes detectable with 8–10 events.
2. **Rain term is fit with one data point** (Dec 19). The 8·tanh saturation curve is plausible but speculative.
3. **Cold lockout based on single observation** (Feb 22–23). The 32°F / 72h threshold is a guess.
4. **Phase 1 construction status unknown.** If curb has been reconstructed to design (+0.04 ft), all thresholds shift up by ~0.5 inch — within noise.
5. **Sea-level rise is accelerating.** Historical-stats project shows
   ~5.45 mm/yr post-1980, vs the long-term 4.05 mm/yr. Will silently
   shift the +0.40 enhancement upward by ~0.07 ft per decade (faster
   than the 0.04 ft/decade long-term rate). Re-calibrate every 5 years,
   not 10.
