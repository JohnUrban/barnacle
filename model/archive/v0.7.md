# Bay Ave Barnacle — Flood Prediction Model v0.7

**Target:** Predict whether 342 Bay Ave (Highlands NJ) will flood in
the next 24 hours, with per-landmark depth estimates at 15 named
landmarks around the property.

**Promotion date:** 2026-06-14 (committed 2026-06-15).

**Changes from v0.6** — driven by three multi-grate spot-check events
(2026-05-18, 2026-05-31, 2026-06-14) all giving the same enhancement
value:

1. **Local enhancement: +0.40 ft → −0.13 ft (constant).** v0.6's
   +0.40 was almost certainly co-fit with the v0.6 rain term — all
   four historical calibration events (Apr 17, Apr 18, Oct 30, Dec 19)
   had rain. Three clean dry-event spot-checks now disagree with
   +0.40 across the entire SH range we've measured (6.17, 6.58, 7.13)
   and all give the same value (~−0.13 ft). See HANDOFF section 9c.3
   and `assets/observations/2026-06-14/README.md` for the full
   analysis.
2. **Single-water-level math.** Rain now adds to a shared water level
   (dZ_rain = 8·tanh(rate)/12 ft), not per-landmark with shedding
   constants. The heat-map already used this; v0.7 makes the depth
   function agree. Removed:
   `d["intersection"] += max(0, rain_add - 2)`,
   `d["lawn_step"] += max(0, rain_add - 4)`,
   `d["porch_step"] += max(0, rain_add - 4)`,
   and per-grate per-landmark additions.
3. **Expanded landmark set: 9 → 15.** Five grates instead of two
   (added `grate_NW`, `grate_SW`, `grate_bay_ave_upstream`); four
   pavement corners as distinct landmarks (`corner_NE`, `corner_NW`,
   `corner_SE`, `corner_SW`).
4. **Renames** (cleanup; not behavior changes):
   - `corner_grate` → **`grate_NE`** (elev also corrected: 3.91 → 3.80)
   - `lowest_sentinel_grate` → **`grate_SE`** (elev unchanged at 3.60)
   - `lowest_road_corner` → **`corner_SE`** (elev unchanged at 3.64)
   - `intersection` → **`intersection_highpoint`** (elev unchanged at 4.54)
5. **`grate_NE` / `grate_NW` elevation corrected**: 3.91 → **3.80**
   per survey PDF (v0.6 had this wrong — `corner_grate` should have
   been the grate elevation, not the pavement-corner elevation; the
   pavement corner at 3.91 is its own landmark now, `corner_NE`).
6. **`grate_SW` elevation refined**: previously 3.55–3.58 placeholder,
   now **3.52** via cross-fit from 3 events (2026-05-18, 2026-05-31,
   2026-06-14).
7. **`grate_bay_ave_upstream`**: established at **3.64 NAVD88**
   (low-point reference; the grate top is uneven 3.64–3.78). Going
   forward, tape from the low point.
8. **Pathway B activation threshold**: shifts from SH 6.33 to **SH
   6.59** (= grate_bay_ave_upstream 3.64 + 2.95 datum/enhancement
   correction).
9. **Negative-surge clip removed**: `forecast_peak = tide_pred +
   max(0.0, surge)` is now `forecast_peak = tide_pred + surge`.
   Anti-surge conditions exist; the forecast is allowed to predict
   below the astronomical tide.
10. **Rain accumulation window**: symmetric ±90 min becomes
    before-biased **[−90 min, +15 min]**. Rain after the peak can't
    raise the peak; the small +15 min forward tolerance covers
    observed flooding lagging predicted peak times by 10–30 min.

v0.6 is preserved at `model/archive/v0.6.md` for reference.

If anything in another document disagrees with v0.7, **v0.7 wins.**

## Core formula

```
# Translate Sandy Hook gauge to local water level at 342 Bay Ave
LOCAL_ENHANCEMENT = -0.13 ft                # 3-event mean (5/18, 5/31, 6/14)
MLLW_TO_NAVD88     = -2.82 ft

water_at_342_NAVD88 = Sandy_Hook_MLLW + LOCAL_ENHANCEMENT + MLLW_TO_NAVD88

# Rain amplification (saturating, applied when peak rain >= 0.1"/hr)
# Single water-level model: rain raises one shared surface uniformly.
if peak_rain_rate_in_hr > 0.1:
    rain_add_ft = (8.0 * tanh(peak_rain_rate_in_hr)) / 12.0
    water_at_342_NAVD88 += rain_add_ft

# Per-landmark depth (inches) — purely geometric from one water level
depth(landmark) = max(0, water_at_342_NAVD88 - landmark.elevation) * 12
```

The shortcut: at any Sandy Hook reading X (MLLW), water at 342 Bay
sits at `(X − 2.95)` ft NAVD88 (without rain). So Sandy Hook
threshold for landmark Y NAVD88 is just `Y + 2.95`.

(For v0.6 this was `Y + 2.42`. The new threshold is +0.53 ft higher
because the enhancement went from +0.40 to −0.13.)

## Three pathways (v0.7 thresholds)

### Pathway A — Direct overtopping (rare, catastrophic)

Bay water rises directly to street and lawn elevations.

- **Onset** (water tops curb at walkway): SH ≥ 7.11 ft MLLW
- **Moderate** (water on lawn step): SH ≥ 7.53 ft
- **Porch step reached** (combined with rain on Oct 30): SH ≥ 8.03 ft
- **Severe** (~1 ft over curb): SH ≥ 8.11 ft
- **Catastrophic** (100-year BFE): SH ≥ 11.0 ft MLLW
- **Sandy 2012**: SH 13.31 ft MLLW peak (6-min) / 12.03 ft hourly

### Pathway B — Storm drain back-flow (the workhorse)

Storm-drain outfalls below Bay Ave become submerged in the bay;
back-pressure pushes water up through inlets onto the street.

The lowest visible grate (`grate_SW` at 3.52, then `grate_SE` at 3.60)
is where this first becomes observable. The Bay Ave upstream grate
(low-point ~3.64) is the actual primary feeder to the user's gutter
at walkway (3.78) — water emerges upstream and runs down to pool at
the gutter.

### Pathway C — Rainfall amplification

Heavy rain coinciding with high tide adds depth via the saturating
term `8 · tanh(rate)` inches, applied as a uniform water-level rise
(`/12` for feet). Trigger threshold unchanged: peak hourly rainfall
≥ 0.1 in/hr.

**The rain term magnitude fits Oct 30 2025 within tape precision as
written.** Re-anchored 2026-06-15 against the photo evidence in
`assets/observations/2025-10-30/`. At the **actual NOAA Oct 30 peak
SH 7.630 ft MLLW** (the early v0.6 spec used 7.57, slightly low) +
1.45 in/hr rain, v0.7 predicts water = **5.28 NAVD88** at peak →
13.4″ at curb, 8.4″ at lawn step, 2.4″ at porch step. The user's
photo at 15:41 ET (+47 min post-peak) shows water 1″ above the
lawn-step elevation on the walkway (4.66 NAVD88); tide-decay
between peak and 15:41 was 0.59 ft, implying **peak water ≥ 5.25
NAVD88** (floor). v0.7's 5.28 is within **0.4″** of that anchor.
The user's original memory ("water at porch first step" = 5.08; "~12
in at curb" = 5.16) both corroborate to within 1–2″. See
`assets/observations/2025-10-30/README.md` for the full reconstruction.

The v0.6 per-landmark rain shedding constants (intersection −2″,
lawn/porch −4″) are removed in v0.7 because they were a different
physical model (isolated puddle shedding) that doesn't apply to the
actual flooding regime at 342 (one connected water body). The
water-is-level math is correct; the rain magnitude (8·tanh(rate))
was preserved from v0.6 and remains correctly calibrated against the
Oct 30 anchor.

## Cold-weather hypothesis (no longer applied as override)

The v0.6 `if mean_air_temp_72h < 32°F and SH < 8.0: depth = 0`
override was demoted on 2026-05-19 to an advisory banner. A 19-event
retrospective found web evidence that ~3 of 5 named-storm candidates
likely flooded Monmouth County despite meeting the override
conditions. The single Feb 22-23 2026 observation that originally
calibrated the rule may be an outlier.

v0.7 retains the `cold_lockout` parameter on `predict_landmark_depths`
for caller-API stability but does NOT zero depths when cold. The
forecast surrounding script renders a yellow advisory banner instead.
See `history/reports/cold_weather_retrospective.md` for the full
analysis.

**Two big lessons from this re-evaluation:**

1. **Landmark-anchored observations are 3–5× more precise than
   depth-at-curb memory.** Dec 19's "covered road except
   intersection high point" pins water to a 2.2″ band; the
   accompanying "7–9 in at curb" memory was inconsistent with the
   landmark anchor and was 5+ inches high. Future spot-checks
   should always record at least one landmark anchor in addition
   to any depth-at-curb estimate. (Going forward this is automatic
   — every spot-check since 5/18 has logged depth-at-named-landmark.)

2. **v0.7 fits the events for which we have landmark or photo
   anchors** (Oct 30 via photo within 0.4″; Dec 19 via landmark
   within 1.7–4.2″ at the most-mismatched end; Apr 17 via the
   "drains overflowing" qualitative match). The remaining gap on
   Apr 18 has no landmark anchor recorded; the "10 in at curb"
   memory is plausibly 5–6″ over-stated, in the same direction
   and roughly the same magnitude as Dec 19's memory drift.

**Bottom line: the original +0.40 enhancement was almost certainly
over-fit to noisy depth-at-curb memories.** The landmark-anchored
observations (Dec 19) and the photo evidence (Oct 30) are both
consistent with v0.7's −0.13 enhancement to within 1–4 inches at
the most-stretched end of the constraint bands. The original +0.40
took the depth memories at face value as the fit target; v0.7
takes the landmark anchors + photo evidence as the more reliable
constraints and accepts the small (≤ 4″) remaining gap as
representing some combination of antecedent moisture, rain-term
form at modest rates, and possible storm-condition effects we
can't yet model.

## What might break this model (and what won't)

1. **Storm-condition enhancement we can't model.** If the original
   +0.40 from Apr 17, 18, Dec 19 was real (not memory imprecision),
   v0.7 will under-predict similar storm-only conditions by 2–8″ at
   curb. We can't distinguish "memory artifact" from "real storm
   amplification" without a tape-measured storm event. Every
   measured storm event tightens this. v0.8 9d.1 framed this way.
2. **Local enhancement extrapolation above SH 7.13** is unverified.
   −0.13 is confirmed across SH 6.17–7.13. Above 7.5, opportunistic
   sanity check.
3. **Rain term**: fits Oct 30 well at peak rate 1.45 in/hr. Whether
   the saturating form `8·tanh(rate)` is right at lower rates (e.g.
   Dec 19's 0.44 in/hr) or with different antecedent moisture is
   open — pairs with HANDOFF 9d.3.
4. **Cold lockout is still an open hypothesis.** v0.7 doesn't apply
   it as override but tracks the events.
5. **Three new grates are tape-measured in only one event each**
   (grate_NW, grate_SW, grate_bay_ave_upstream). Cross-grate
   consistency on 6/14 confirms the elevations are right to within
   0.5–1″, but more events will tighten.
6. **`grate_bay_ave_upstream` uses the low-point reference (3.64)**;
   the grate top is uneven over 1.7″ vertical span. Measurements
   referenced to a different point on the same grate will read
   differently.
7. **`lawn_step` and `porch_step` elevations are still inferred**.
   HANDOFF 7.4 queues cross-fit refinement from any future event
   where water reaches them.
8. **Sea-level rise is accelerating.** Historical-stats project
   shows ~5.45 mm/yr post-1980. Will silently shift the enhancement
   away from −0.13 (probably toward more negative — bay relative to
   gauge) over time. Re-calibrate every 5 years.
