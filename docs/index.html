What each term means

Sandy Hook peak (MLLW)
The forecast peak water height at the NOAA Sandy Hook gauge (station 8531680) for the worst high tide in the next 24 hours, in feet above Mean Lower Low Water. This is the live input that changes tide to tide — it already folds in the astronomical tide plus any storm surge.
Local enhancement (+0.40 ft)
A fixed bump because 342 Bay Ave runs higher than the gauge reads, fit from past street-flooding events. It is most likely a storm-surge propagation effect rather than a true constant; v0.7 will test whether it should scale with surge instead of being flat.
MLLW→NAVD88 offset (−2.82 ft)
A pure datum conversion. The gauge reports heights in MLLW; the landmark elevations at the house were surveyed in NAVD88. This is fixed geometry, not a tunable model parameter.
Peak rain (in/hr)
The heaviest single hour of rainfall the NWS forecasts in the window around the high tide. Rain adds depth through a saturating curve, 8·tanh(rate) inches, and only kicks in above 0.1 in/hr. The crowned intersection sheds 2″ of it and the lawn/porch shed 4″ (a v0.6 quirk that v0.7 replaces with a uniform rise).

The whole model: convert the gauge's tide forecast to a water level at the house (first three terms), then at each landmark the depth is simply how far that water level sits above the landmark's surveyed elevation, with a rain term added on top. Everything downstream — the alerts, the heat-map, the depth table — is this one calculation applied at different points.

Regime glossary

The single word in the subject line (DRY / STREET / LIGHT / MODERATE / SEVERE) summarises severity based on water depth at the curb.

  • dry — no visible water at any landmark
  • street — water on the street at sub-curb landmarks; curb still dry
  • light — water at curb (0–4 inches)
  • moderate — water at curb (4–8 inches)
  • severe — water past lawn step / bulkhead overtopping territory
  • cold_lockout — cold conditions met but rule no longer actively applied (see retrospective)
